Bioinformatics Content — Overview¶

Six lectures covering the biological and data science foundations of the Master’s Programme in Bioinformatics, led by Nathaniel Street (nathaniel.street@umu.se, Department of Plant Physiology, Umeå University).

Course: 5BI00A Computing for Data-Driven Biology · 31 August – 29 September 2026

Pre-HPC2N lectures¶

# Title Description
00 Programme Introduction Programme overview, teaching philosophy, two-year structure, career pathways
01 What is Bioinformatics? Origins, interdisciplinarity, epistemic limitations, AI tools, programme ILOs

These two sessions are delivered before the HPC2N computing environment block.

Post-HPC2N lectures¶

These sessions follow the HPC2N block (sections 1–10) and assume you can use the Linux command line, Git, and Slurm.

# Title Key topics ILOs
11 Biological Database Landscape INSDC, UniProt, GO, functional and specialist databases 1, 4, 5, 7, 12
12 BLAST and Sequence Similarity Homology, E-values, BLOSUM, web/API/local workflows 1, 5, 7, 9, 12
13 Bioinformatics File Formats FASTA, FASTQ, SAM/BAM, VCF, GFF/GTF, BED 1, 4, 7, 9
14 FAIR and Open Science FAIR principles, reproducibility, open science 3, 13
15 FAIR in Practice PlantGenIE API, GEO metadata, essay briefing 1, 3, 7, 12, 13

Assessment¶

FAIR compliance essay · 1,200–1,500 words · Deadline: 29 September 2026
See Lecture 14 for the assignment brief and marking rubric.
